MIRAJ  ISLAMIC  SCHOOL

NEWS

    

By Anver Saad

NewYork, June 2, 2000

Seerat-Un-Nabi conference

THE Seerat-Un-Nabi conference and fundraiser for schools drawn good representation from lovers of Prophet Muhammad (p.b.u.h.) and education. Muslims from the New York-tri-state and Long Island were conming before Asr on Saturday night to attend this gathering for the praise of Muhammad (p@bu.h.) and for their commitments for the education of Muslim kids and youths.
The Quran was recited in the beautiful  voice of an Albanian Muslim Zulqarnain, Vardar who is also the Imam and Director of Albanian Islamic center, Staten Island and the program started with the welcome address of Dr. Safdar Chadda.
Students of different schools have given beautiful presentations. Nasheed/Hadiths and poems in a very good style were presented by the Students of the Muslim Center Elementary School and students of Miraj School. The Quran and poem recited by Students of Ideal Islamic School and Islamic Elementary school. Awn Abbas, a student of grade II of Al- Iman School gave a speech on "I am Fortunate to be in an Islamic School," and Mahjabeen Motiwwala spoke on the topic "How, an Islamic School has helped me."
Dr. Muzaffar Partowmah explained about "the future of Islam in North America." He introduced, the works of Islam from different levels and groups in North America and what is going on in organizational, publication, and educational fields.,
Shamsheer Ali Beg, Principal of Crescent School highlighted the importance of Islamic Schools. He said that for the moralization of the society we have to build Masajid and schools. Mohammad Moutaz Charaf, a Syrian Muslim, Engineer of IBM and Khateeb of Darul Islah talked 'in a very informative way on the topic" Prophet and Children."

 

Dr. Farooq Khan lectured on 'Politics in Islam." It was a very good presentation in which It discussed the political strategy of Prophet Muhammad (p.b.u.h) who worked in different circumstances and with different possibilities and alternatives. After Maghrip Prayer principals and teachers of different school talked about their responsibilities, teaching methodologies and work. Rafeek Mohamed, Principal of Islamic Elementary School, Shenhan Khalil and Aydin Muzace of Razi School talked on this subject Istefa Naqvi, secretary Muslim Unity conducted the grant presentations to school. The program concluded with  the thanks of Naheed Farooqi, Principal, Muslim Central Elementary School and with a dua by Ghazi Khmkan, Dhwtor Communication, Islamic Center, Westbury.
