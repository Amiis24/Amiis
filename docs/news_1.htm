NewYork, N.Y, 1 June 2000

Saving Our Youths Should Be Our National Priority

Imam Zurkani Vardari speech (1st Row most right)

It is short sighted and naive to think that if we break the television set and ban Western music that our kids will be saved from the perils of the popular Western culture, although these measures maybe of some  benefit if taken early enough in the child's life. What can we do? One answer is the Islamic education for all Muslim children, in a safe and moral environment. Full time Islamic schools are one of the clear options we have available for us for this purpose. But we have to compete with well-funded public schools and lavishly equipped schools run by other religious communities," said Dr. Safdar H. Chadda, Director of the Muslim Unity Foundation Inc. while addressing a well attended Seerat-Un-Nabi Conference at the Roosevelt Hotel.


Most of our children are becoming victims to the popular American culture. They often listen to the music of "hard rock" groups and rap artists. They read magazines such as "Vibe" and "Source"; watch videos of contemporary pop artists and see movies that contain exten- sive violence and sexual content, said Dr. Chadda.


Dr. Chadda quoted Dr. Dobson, an American writer and radio commentator, who said that " unfortunately, those spiritual concepts on which this nation was built are being superseded now by philosophies and judgements that are rooted in atheism. The name of Ciod has been removed from every vestige of public life, as though He were a cancerous growth that threatened the life of the organism. This situation is critical and must be addressed because the future of the Muslim community is in jeopardy. While I do not condemn all aspects of the popular Western culture, I do understand its inherent dangers, particularly to Muslim  youth. Although young Muslim are expected to live moral and ethical lives, there are limited cultural alternatives to counter act negative influences, Dr. Chadda added.


Muslim presence in the West of- fm Muslims and the West a unique opportunity to understand each other, come into serious existential dialogue, and produce a model community for the rest of humanity, Dr. Chadda explained.
In conclusion Dr. Chadda said that the Muslim Unity Foundation (MUF) is a nonprofit organization whichhas taken an initiative to sup- port Muslim full-time schools fi- nancially. These schools may pro- vide a safe and supportive environment for our kids. Saving our youth should be our national priority. We have taken on this gigantous challenge and need your moral and financial support. Let us join together to save our future genemtions.
This Secrat-Un-Nabi Conference was organized by the Muslim Unity Foundation, which was established last year with a main objective to make Muslim full time schools financiafly stronger to give quality education, and to admit maximum number of students to make them good citizens of the United States. The conference was addressed by Imam Ayoub Abdul Eaqi of Masjid Al-Tawbeed; Mr. Mohammed Cheriff, Khateeb Dar-ul-Islah, N.J.; Mrs. Aisha Al-Adawiya, a polifi- cal activist, Dr. Muzaffar Panowmah, a human rights active ist; Mr. Shamsbeer Ali Raig, Mem- ber of the Board of Trustees of the Muslim Unity Foundation; Dr. Farooq Khan, spokesman for the Islamic Center of Long Island, Inom Rafeeq Mohammad, Principal of the Islamic Elementary School; Mr. Abu Zehm Principal of the Razi School; and Mr. Istefa Naqvi, Secretary and Member of the Board of Trustees of the Muslim Unity Foundation.

On this occasion, the Muslim Unity Foundation handed over a check of $750.00 each to the principals of the participating schools. Those schools are as follows:
Crescent School, Al-iman School, Muslim Center Elementary School, Razi School, Islamic Elementary School, Ideal School, Miraj School and the American Islamic Acad- emy.
Mr. Nasir Ali Akbar, Principal of Al-Iman School was the Master of Ceremony on this occasion. Mrs. Nabeed Famoqi, Principal of the Muslim Center Elementary School gave a vote of thanks, and Alhaj Ghazi Khankan, Director Inter- faith, Islamic Center of Long Island offered a concluding Dua.


Immediately after Maghnb prayer the attendees received refreshment packets which were prepared by Joy Restaurant in Manhanan.
